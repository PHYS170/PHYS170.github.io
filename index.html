Elastic Collision

During an elastic collision, the total momentum and kinetic energy is conserved. As a result of the collision, the objects' velocities change proportionally to their mass.


Inelastic Collision

During an inelastic collision, the total momentum is conserved, but the total kinetic energy is not. Kinetic energy that is lost is converted into other forms of energy such as thermal or sound energy.
